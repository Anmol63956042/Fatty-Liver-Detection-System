What is Fatty Liver Disease?

A growing epidemic affecting over 25% of the global population — often completely silent until advanced stages

NAFLD (Non-Alcoholic)

Fat accumulation in liver cells not caused by alcohol. Strongly linked to obesity, Type 2 diabetes, and metabolic syndrome.

🍺

ALD (Alcoholic)

Caused by excessive alcohol consumption. Ranges from simple steatosis to alcoholic hepatitis and cirrhosis.

⚠

NASH (Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis)

An advanced form of NAFLD with active liver inflammation and cell damage — a precursor to cirrhosis and liver cancer.

📊

Silent Progression

Most patients experience no symptoms. By the time symptoms appear (fatigue, abdominal pain), liver damage may already be significant.

How the AI System Works

A transparent, step-by-step pipeline from raw clinical data to explainable diagnosis

Data Input

Patient's 10 biochemical and demographic parameters are entered and validated against acceptable clinical ranges.

Preprocessing

Gender encoding, median imputation of missing values, and StandardScaler Z-score normalisation applied identically to training.

Random Forest

200-tree ensemble constructs a probability from learned decision boundaries across multiple clinical parameter combinations.

Neural Network

4-layer ANN with BatchNorm and Dropout produces a second independent probability using deep feature representations.

Ensemble Fusion

Both model probabilities are averaged to produce a final confidence score — more robust than any single model alone.

SHAP Explanation

TreeExplainer calculates each feature's contribution (positive or negative) to the specific prediction — no black box.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the system, the disease, and AI-assisted diagnosis

Is this a replacement for a doctor's diagnosis?
No. LiverAI is a clinical decision support tool. It assists healthcare professionals by flagging high-risk patients and explaining contributing factors. It does not replace clinical examination, imaging, or specialist consultation.
What dataset was used to train the model?
The model was trained on the Indian Liver Patient Dataset (ILPD), containing 30000 patient records with 10 clinical parameters collected from hospitals in Andhra Pradesh, India.
Why are two models used together?
Random Forest and ANN each capture different patterns in data. Averaging their probabilities (ensemble learning) reduces individual model variance and consistently outperforms either model alone on unseen data.
What does a SHAP value mean?
A positive SHAP value means that feature pushed the prediction toward disease. A negative value means it pushed toward no disease. The magnitude shows how strongly it influenced the final prediction for that specific patient.
Can fatty liver disease be reversed?
Yes — in early stages (simple steatosis and early NASH), fatty liver is fully reversible through weight loss (5–10% body weight), exercise, elimination of alcohol, and dietary changes. Early detection is therefore critical.
